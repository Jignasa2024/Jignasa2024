"Workshop on High Performance computing"

High-Performance Computing (HPC) now becomes one of the emerging technologies. It was designed to educate and provide aspects of parallel computing. HPC uses super computers and parallel processing techniques to solve compute intensive tasks. HPC has become essential in various fields requiring significant computational power for tasks such as simulations, data analysis, machine learning, and scientific research. The focus of the program to introduce the fundamentals of parallel computing including parallel architectures, programming models and commonly used parallel programming constructs/libraries/languages

Paper Presentation

The Paper Presentation event will give participants an opportunity to present their research, ideas, or innovative solutions on a chosen theme. Each participant/team will prepare a paper on a topic related to technology, science, or engineering and present their findings using a presentation. Judges will evaluate the papers based on originality, content, clarity, and presentation skills.

"Circuit Debugger"

The Circuit Debugging event is a competitive challenge where participants are given faulty electronic circuits. They must analyze, troubleshoot, and correct errors within a limited time. The event tests participants' knowledge of circuit theory, practical skills, and quick problem-solving abilities. Judges will evaluate based on accuracy and time taken to fix the circuit.

Trail Run

“TRIAL RUN” is a mock interview for college students which is a simulated interview experience designed to help them prepare for real job or internship interviews. It mimics the format and questions of a professional interview, allowing students to practice answering common interview questions, improving their communication skills, and receiving feedback on their performance. This practice helps build confidence, reduce interview anxiety, and enhance their chances of success in actual interview.
